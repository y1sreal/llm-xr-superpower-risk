Overview

CHI 2026

Comprehensive analysis of LLM × XR integration

The integration of generative AI with Extended Reality (XR) technologies has unlocked unprecedented capabilities, empowering users with enhanced cognitive, sensory, and environmental control—effectively enabling "superpowers" in immersive digital spaces.

This literature review examines both the transformative benefits and potential risks of LLM-enabled XR systems across healthcare, education, professional training, and beyond. By analyzing 135 papers, we identify three superpower categories and seven risk dimensions that characterize this rapidly evolving field.

Key Insights

Core findings from the literature review

Dual-Edged Nature

Every superpower category comes with associated risks. The most powerful capabilities often present the greatest vulnerabilities, particularly in behavioral manipulation and data privacy.

Dominant Concerns

Manipulation of thoughts and behavior is the most prevalent risk, affecting 100 papers (74%), followed by user data awareness issues in 60 papers (44%).

Application Domains

LLM-enabled XR shows particular promise in healthcare, education, and professional training, where cognitive and sensory enhancements can significantly improve outcomes.

Need for Standards

The rapid evolution of these technologies has outpaced the development of ethical guidelines, industry standards, and safeguarding measures for vulnerable populations.

Mind Reading Capabilities

Multimodal behavioral signal interpretation (eye, body, voice) presents unique opportunities for enhanced social cognition but raises significant privacy concerns about pervasive data collection.

Future Directions

Design guidelines must balance innovation with user protection, emphasizing transparency, consent, and ethical deployment of AI-powered XR technologies.
